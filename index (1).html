About Me

I'm Saksham Sharma — a Data Analyst and AI practitioner who believes the most powerful thing you can do with data is make it impossible to ignore.

Currently in my final year of BE Computer Science (Big Data Analytics) at Chandigarh University, I've spent the last three years doing something most analysts don't — moving fluidly between the boardroom metric and the model weight. I build things end-to-end: from raw SQL queries that surface hidden business risk, all the way to Graph-RAG architectures published in peer-reviewed research.

My work has found $2.71M in unsold inventory nobody had quantified, exposed a dangerous 65% single-vendor dependency in a supply chain, and achieved Recall@5 of 0.88 on a legal contract AI benchmark — results that sit in two published papers, one in IEEE Xplore and one in a Scopus-indexed Springer conference.

What drives me is the gap between data that exists and decisions it should be driving. I work in Python, SQL, and Power BI daily — but I've also built with Neo4j knowledge graphs, LangChain pipelines, FAISS vector stores, and Gemini LLMs. Three industry certifications from Google, Microsoft, and Oracle anchor the fundamentals. The research keeps pushing the frontier.

I'm not looking to just run reports. I want to build systems that change how organizations think — one insight at a time.

Graph-RAG for Legal Contract Analysis

A novel Graph-RAG framework combining knowledge graphs and vector search for explainable multi-hop reasoning across complex legal contracts — outperforming standard RAG baselines.

  • Achieved Precision@5: 0.76 and Recall@5: 0.88 on the CUAD legal benchmark dataset
  • Benchmarked vs. Vector-RAG, demonstrating measurable reasoning improvements
